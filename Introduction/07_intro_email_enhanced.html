Email Security

Introduction Level
Back to Level 1

Email security helps users recognize suspicious messages, verify requests before acting, and report threats quickly so they can be contained.

Narrative 01

Recognize the Threat

Suspicious emails often contain warning signs.

What it means

Attackers make messages look familiar so users act quickly. Watch for unusual senders, pressure, misspellings, unexpected files, fake login pages, and requests involving money or credentials.

Narrative 02

Verify Before You Trust

Verification breaks many email attacks.

What it means

Use a known phone number, known website, or internal process to verify unusual requests. Do not rely on contact details inside the suspicious email.

Narrative 03

Stop the Compromise

Layered controls help when a user clicks.

What it means

One click does not have to become a breach. Security tools can block malicious pages, stop attachments, detect unusual login attempts, and limit account takeover.

Narrative 04

Report and Respond

Fast reporting protects the whole organization.

What it means

When suspicious emails are reported quickly, security teams can investigate, remove copies from other mailboxes, block links, and warn affected users.
